Introduction

A smartwatch is a wearable computer in the form of a watch; modern smartwatches provide a local touchscreen interface for daily use, while an associated smartphone app provides for management and telemetry (such as long-term biomonitoring). While early models could perform basic tasks, such as calculations, digital time telling, translations, and game-playing, 2010s smartwatches have more general functionality closer to smartphones, including mobile apps, a mobile operating system and WiFi/Bluetooth connectivity.

Features

The smartwatch market is heavily saturated. As a result, manufacturers try to evolve existing features and implement new ones in an effort to stay ahead of competitors. This constant evolution is a plus for the consumer, as a wide array of features become available in smartwatches.
